How do you perceive workload of crowdsourcing HITs?

Some people feel that mental or temporal demands are the essential aspects of workload regardless of the effort they spent on a given task or the level of performance they achieved. Others feel that if they performed well the workload must have been low and if they performed badly it must have been high. We want to know what your perspective is.

Please carefully read the definitions for the following rating scale

They are essential for understanding your duty and for properly answering the questions. .

 
Consider the typical HIT that you perform in MTurk...

Mental Demand

Refers to:

For performing the typical HIT in MTurk, how much mental and perceptual activity is required (thinking, deciding, calculating, remembering, looking, searching, etc.) ? Is the typical HIT easy or demanding, simple or complex?

Physical Demand

Refers to:

For performing the typical HIT in MTurk, how much physical activity is required (clicking, typing, scrolling, copying and pasting, etc.)? Is the typical HIT easy or demanding, restful or laborious?

Temporal Demand

Refers to:

When performing the typical HIT in MTurk, how much time pressure do you usually feel due to the rate or pace at which the HIT occurs? Is the pace of the typical HIT slow and leisurely or rapid and frantic? Do you have time stress when perfoming it?

Performance

Refers to:

How successful do you think you are in accomplishing the goals of the typical HITs set by the requester (or yourself)? How satisfied are you with your performance in accomplishing these goals?

Effort

Refers to:

It refers to both mental and physical effort needed to accomplish expected level of performance in HITs.

Frustration Level

Refers to:

How insecure, discouraged, irritated, stressed, and annoyed versus secure, gratified, content, relaxed, and complacent do you feel while performing the typical HIT?

 
Now, you should assess the relative importance of the above mentioned six factors in workload you experience when performing the typically HIT.

The procedure is simple: You will be presented with a series of pairs of scale (for example, Effort vs. Mental Demands). You should choose which item in the pair is more important contributor to your experience of workload when performing a typical HIT.
